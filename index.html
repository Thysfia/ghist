What Geology Studies


Geology reveals the processes that have shaped the landscape of the Earth, whether in the present or in the past. With geology, we can learn how the diverse rocks and minerals on our planet were formed and distributed. We can also study how rocks and fossils can be used to explain past climate change, and why learning from the past is the key to reach a sustainable future.


(British Geological Survey.)

The Best Subject Combos to Study Geology

Physics

Needed for seismology, tectonics, geophysics, earthquakes, and waves in rocks.

Chemistry

Very important for mineral identification, rock formation, reactions, geochemistry.

Mathematics

Used for modeling Earth processes, analyzing data, slope stability, erosion rates, etc.

Geography

Directly connected: landforms, climate, maps, GIS, Earth systems.

English

For research papers, scholarships, international programs.

Structure of Earth


Earth is not just a solid ball—or sphere, to be exact. It consists of several layers, each of which is different. There are two ways to divide the layers: chemically, and mechanically.
